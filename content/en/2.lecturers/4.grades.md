The Student Grades module is designed to handle continuous assessment according to ECTS standards, specifically managing the **60% Workload total**.

## Module Selection and Grade Entry Prerequisites

Before a lecturer can enter grades, two conditions must be met for a module to become gradeable:

1. **Workload Approval**: The workload for the module must be approved. This approval can be granted either by the **lecturer themselves** or by the **department admin**, depending on the institution's workflow.
2. **Module Not Locked**: The module must not be locked by the **Exam Committee**. Once the Exam Committee locks a module, no further grade entries or modifications are permitted.

Only when both conditions are satisfied will the module become available for grade entry.

![Module Selection and Prerequisites](en/lecturer/a-moudle-can-grade.png)

## Workload Status Table

When a lecturer selects a module, the workload is presented as a **summary table**, where each workload item (Quizzes, Reports, Projects, Mid-term, Homework) appears as a row with its title.

The table uses a visual indicator to communicate the status of each workload item:

- **Blue title (clickable)**: Both conditions above are met. The lecturer can click the title to navigate directly to the grade entry page for that specific workload item.
- **Grey title (not clickable)**: One or both conditions are not yet satisfied. The item is either pending workload approval or has been locked by the Exam Committee.

This design ensures that lecturers are guided clearly toward items that are ready for grading, without exposing locked or unapproved items for editing.

### Navigating to Grade Entry

Clicking a **blue workload title** takes the lecturer to a dedicated grade entry page for that item. From there, grades can be entered and the system will automatically handle the ECTS weighting calculations as described below.

![Grade Entry Page Navigation]()

## Assessment Weights

The grading interface includes specific columns for different assessment types with pre-defined weights:

- **Quizzes**: Weighted at **8%**.
- **Reports and Projects**: Each component is weighted at **16%**.
- **Mid-term Exam**: Weighted at **24%**.
- **Homework**: Options for multiple assignments.

## Automated Grade Scaling

The system simplifies grade entry by allowing lecturers to edit the **"Grade From"** value for any assessment. You can enter the raw score from your specific test (e.g., a quiz out of 10 or a midterm out of 50), and the system will automatically calculate the corresponding ECTS-weighted grade for the final workload total.

### Automated Grade Scaling and "Grade From"

A key feature of the Student Grades page is its automated calculation system, which simplifies how you enter and manage academic results. The system is designed to remove mathematical complexity for the lecturer. When setting up assessments (such as Quizzes, Reports, or Mid-terms), you can specify the **"Grade From"** value (the maximum raw score possible for that specific task).

- **Ease of Input**: You enter the student's actual marks based on your specific test or assignment score (e.g., a quiz out of 20 or a report out of 10).
- **Automatic Weighting**: The system then automatically performs the necessary calculations to scale that raw score to fit the required percentage weight for the module. For example, if a Quiz is weighted at 8% and a Report at 16%, the system converts your raw input into these final values instantly.

## Workload Management (60%)

The grading structure is divided into a **"Workload"** portion and a final examination portion.

- **The 60% Workload**: The various continuous assessments you manage on this page—including Homework, Quizzes, Projects, and Mid-terms—collectively account for **60%** of the total student grade (the "Workload" total).
- **System Integrity**: As you input different marks, the system dynamically updates the total workload score to ensure it accurately reflects the 60% weight assigned to these activities. You can monitor this progress through the "Workload" status icons in the "My Subjects" module.

This automation ensures that you can focus on evaluation while the system handles the arithmetic required to remain compliant with the Bologna system's ECTS standards.
