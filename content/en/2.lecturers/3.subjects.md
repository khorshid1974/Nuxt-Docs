# EPU Bologna System: My Subjects & Workload Management

This documentation describes how lecturers manage their assigned courses and configure the ECTS workload required to enable student grade entry.

---

## 1. My Subjects (بابەتەکانم)

Every lecturer has access to a primary menu item called **My Subjects (بابەتەکانم)**. This section serves as the central directory for your teaching assignments.

### Subject List View
The page displays a table of all modules you are responsible for, including:
*   **Subject Name:** (e.g., Computer Principles/1).
*   **Academic Details:** College, department, group, and semester.
*   **Workload Icon:** A blue clipboard icon used to access the workload settings for that specific course.

---

## 2. Managing Course Workload

Clicking the blue icon for a subject opens the **ECTS Workload Calculation Form**. This form is used to define the total student effort hours for the semester.

### Editable Workload
When a workload is in its initial state, it is fully **editable**. Lecturers can:
*   **Add Activities:** Click the green **"+"** button to add new rows for different academic tasks.
*   **Set Time Factors:** Enter the **Time Factor** (hours) and frequency (**No**) for Course activities, Assignments, and Assessments.
*   **Remove Activities:** Use the red **trash/delete** icons to remove unnecessary rows.

![Editable Workload Placeholder](/kr/lecturers/editable-workload.png)

---

## 3. Workload Confirmation (جێگیرکردن)

The **Confirmation (جێگیرکردن)** step is a critical administrative milestone in the system.

*   **Action:** Once the workload calculations are complete and the ECTS values are correct, the configuration must be confirmed using the **"جێگیرکردن"** button.
*   **Authority:** According to system rules, this confirmation can be performed either by the **lecturer** or by their **department**.
*   **Enabling Grades:** A confirmed workload is a prerequisite for entering academic data. **The lecturer will only be able to enter student grades once the workload has been confirmed.**

---

## 4. Modifying Confirmed Workloads

Once a workload is confirmed, the system locks the data to ensure academic integrity. The status will change to **"Accepted"**, and the input fields will be disabled.

### Removal of Confirmation
*   **Authority:** The lecturer cannot unilaterally unlock a confirmed workload. **Removing the confirmation status to allow further editing can be done by the Department only.**
*   **Data Risk Warning:** 
> [!CAUTION]
> **Warning:** Removing the confirmation of a workload to make edits may cause the **permanent loss of all student grades** already entered for that subject.
